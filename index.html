About me

Born on June 27, 1869, in Lithuania, I experienced the czar's Anti-Semitic policies and economic instability, which forced me and my family to move from Lithuania to Prussia and then to Russia in search of economic stability. These displacements and my distaste for the role of women in traditional Jewish families led me to immigrate to America when I was sixteen. While working as a garment worker became involved in the labor movement. Most tellingly, the trial, conviction, and execution, on specious evidence, of a group of anarchists for an 1886 bombing in Haymarket Square in Chicago inspired me to become an anarchist activist.
